Dibyajyoti Mohanta

Postdoctoral Researcher · BioSimLab, University at Buffalo

I am a postdoctoral researcher trained in statistical physics, with over eight years of experience studying macromolecules — DNA, polymers, proteins, and RNA — using computer simulation and numerical methods.

Current research interests

  1. Dynamics of biocondensates
  2. Optical DNA mapping (time-series analysis, fluorescence imaging)
  3. Polymer collapse, adsorption, and migration under confinement
  4. DNA melting in relation to forensic studies

Journey so far

From a B.Sc. in Physics at Asutosh College (University of Calcutta) to a Ph.D. at IIT (BHU) and postdoctoral work at Lund University, Sweden, and now at the University at Buffalo — my work bridges statistical mechanics with the biology of long molecules.
