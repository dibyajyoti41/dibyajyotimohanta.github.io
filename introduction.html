Introduction

A short biographical note on training, research, and the road that led to RNA condensates.

Welcome to my page. I graduated with a B.Sc. (Honours) in Physics from Asutosh College, University of Calcutta in 2011, and went on to complete my M.Sc. in Physics at St. Xavier's College, University of Calcutta in 2013, specialising in solid-state physics. I also earned a Bachelor of Education in Physics and Mathematics from Kurukshetra University in 2014.

I joined the Physics Ph.D. program at IIT (BHU), Varanasi in 2015 and defended my thesis in December 2021 under the supervision of Prof. Debaprasad Giri. After six months as a researcher at IMSc Chennai, I joined Dr. Tobias Ambjörnsson's lab as a postdoc at CEC, Lund University in August 2022. There I worked extensively on software for bacterial DNA species identification by analysing fluorescence DNA images — sequential sampling of ligands, theoretical probability distributions of camera images, improving theoretical barcodes, and DNA-melting-mediated twin differentiation.

In August 2024 I joined Hung Nguyen's BioSimLab at the University at Buffalo, where I now work on the dynamics and aging behaviour of pathological RNA condensates, a topic with deep implications for neurodegenerative disease.
